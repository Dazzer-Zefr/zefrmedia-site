Our iGaming History

We Have the Experience
& the Story

A wild ride that's only getting better. From one of the world's first online casinos in 1995, to the top-ranked sportsbook in North America, to an AI-native platform built for what's next — three decades at the frontier of iGaming.

The Pioneers
1995 — 2003
Before “iGaming” was even a word, we were building it — from a Pacific island with a 256K line.
1995

First online gaming license, Rarotonga

Acquired one of the world's first online gaming licenses in the Cook Islands and formed Casinos of the South Pacific. Kia Orana, Cook Islands.

1995

We brought the internet to an island

As part of the licensing deal we worked with Cook Islands Telecom and NZ Telecom to establish the country's first internet connection — running on a 256K line at $25,000/month while calls ran $9.99/minute.

1996

One of the world's first casino games

Casinos of the South Pacific launched one of the first real online casino games anywhere.

CNET — Virtual casinos bet big ↗
1997

A publicly traded iGaming company

Launched NetBet — well before the current NetBet entity acquired the name and built its own business.

LA Times — Irvine's NetBet.com ↗
1999

On to Costa Rica

Bandwidth constraints moved operations to Costa Rica — leaving behind lifelong friendships. Meitaki, Cook Islands.

2002

The Wire Act ruling

It was determined the Federal Wire Act did not restrict online gambling.

2003

A $2.6B industry

The iGaming industry was valued at roughly $2.6 billion — still in its infancy.

The Rise
2004 — 2010
We built betED into the top-ranked North American sportsbook — and shipped the interface every major book would later copy.
Industry first
2004

betED launches — and changes the category

After extensive research, betED launched the world's first Flash-based sports-betting interface, revolutionizing one-page wagering. As seen on NBC, USA Today and the Chicago Tribune — the format was later replicated by every major sportsbook.

2005

Zefr Media Inc. is founded

Launched to provide a full suite of iGaming services — software development, eCommerce, marketing and customer service.

2006

Profit-sharing with Covers.com

Formed a lucrative partnership with Covers.com / SportsDirect Inc. — the authority in sports-betting information — alongside veterans Paul Lavers and Joe McDonald, a proven formula that helped build Bodog's brand presence.

#1 in North America
2009

betED hits #1 in North America

betED grew to the leading sportsbook in North America, holding the number-one spot on Covers.com — ranked above names still recognized today.

Industry first
2010

One of the world's first live-play interfaces

Launched one of the first in-play / live betting interfaces. Live-play now accounts for as much as 80% of sports-betting revenue. Once again, at the forefront.

Tested & Tempered
2011 — 2018
A government sweep — not the market — ended an era. We were #1 when it happened. What we lost in operations, we gained in experience.
The turning point
2011

Black Friday — a forced evolution

In a sweeping move to eliminate US competition, betED was shut down alongside PokerStars, Full Tilt and others — “Black Friday.” We weren't beaten in the market; we were #1 on Covers.com when prohibition returned. The companies fell, but the operators' knowledge didn't — it's exactly what Zefr is built on today.

2012

The power of money

PokerStars settled for $731 million with no admission of wrongdoing — and was explicitly permitted to re-enter regulated US markets later. It did.

Reuters — Poker companies settle ↗
2015

Covers.com acquired for $54M

Gracenote Media (Tribune, later Nexstar) acquired Covers.com — a business built largely on profit-sharing with offshore books. Proof that major US media would embrace what we'd helped pioneer.

2017

An industry we know, validated

Stake.com launched and within six years became one of the largest iGaming companies on earth — the same software-plus-service formula behind betED. Founder Darren Wright also completed his MBA.

2018

Prohibition ends

The Supreme Court struck down the 1992 law that effectively banned commercial sports betting — opening the US market that flourishes today.

Forbes — Winners of the SCOTUS decision ↗
