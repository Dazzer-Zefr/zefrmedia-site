The Shift

From election novelty
to betting's parallel market.

In eighteen months, event contracts went from a regulatory curiosity to a market trading around $24 billion a month — with sports, not politics, driving it. The exchanges hold federal designations, the NYSE's parent company has put $2 billion behind Polymarket, and sportsbooks are answering in kind: DraftKings bought an exchange, FanDuel partnered with CME. This isn't adjacent to betting anymore. It's converging with it.

The Product

Yes or no. Priced in cents.

Every market is a binary contract: buy YES or NO between 1¢ and 99¢, where the price is the probability. There's no overround to manage — the exchange takes a fee and the crowd makes the price. It plays like betting on the surface and clears like a derivative underneath, and the same instrument covers sports, macro, politics and culture alike.

Routes to Market

Three ways in. Three different businesses.

Distribute means brokering access into an existing exchange — the fastest route live, and the thinnest cut. Embed means a white-label or API module such as Matchbook Predictions or Shift Markets running inside your wallet and KYC: your brand, provided liquidity, no trading desk. Own means acquiring or building the exchange itself — the DraftKings–Railbird and Robinhood–MIAXdx play: full margin, full regulatory weight. Which fits is a licensing, capital and cannibalisation question — not a product one.

Regulation

Financial rails or gaming rails.
The gating decision.

In the US, event contracts trade on federally designated contract markets — rails several states are contesting in court. In Europe, Gibraltar updated its Gambling Act and licensed the first prediction market under a gaming framework. Same product, two legal theories. Your jurisdiction strategy decides which one applies to you.

⚖

Federal Rails — US

CFTC-designated contract markets and clearing organisations, with federal jurisdiction asserted over state gambling law — and state challenges still live.

🌍

Gaming Rails — International

Gibraltar licensed Europe's first prediction market under its gaming framework; other iGaming jurisdictions can host event betting under existing licences.
